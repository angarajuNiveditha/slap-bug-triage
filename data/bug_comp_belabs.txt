Subject: VTON: Wrong gender draping — female style drops shown to male users in Social Finds feed

Hi,

There is a gender mismatch bug in the VTON feature within the Social Finds feed.
Male users are consistently seeing female Style Drops content and female VTON
draping overlays on product cards inside the feed. The decoded looks cards are
also showing women's outfit recommendations to male-profiled accounts.

This is a BE_Labs / personalization pipeline issue. The persona gender attribute
(personaAddedAt field) is not being read correctly during the journey continuation
flow, causing the wrong VTON model to be loaded.

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Log in with a male-profiled account (gender = Male in profile)
2. Navigate to the Social Finds feed
3. Scroll through decoded looks or VTON product cards
4. Tap "Try it on" on any product
5. Observe the VTON draping overlay

Expected: Male VTON model is loaded; outfit recommendations are gender-appropriate.
Actual: Female VTON draping model is used. Style Drops shows women's fashion.
        Decoded Looks cards suggest women's outfits to male users.

Reproducibility: 100% consistent for male accounts after the personaAddedAt
                 migration last week.

Impact:
All male users in Social Finds feed are seeing wrong VTON gender draping and
wrong Style Drops recommendations. Poor experience — users are likely to dismiss
the feature entirely. Affects the full feed personalization pipeline.

Environment:
- Platform: Android, iOS (both affected)
- App Version: 2.4.2
- Feature: VTON / Social Finds / Decoded Looks / Style Drops (BE_Labs)
- Root cause suspect: personaAddedAt field not propagated after DAO migration
